San Carlos: A Foodie's Paradise by the Sea

Fine Dining • International Flavors • Coastal Elegance

Nestled along the Sea of Cortez, San Carlos, Sonora, offers more than just stunning coastal views — it's a place where seafood, international flavors, and local tradition meet. Whether you're strolling the marina, lounging on a beach, or exploring the local streets, a culinary adventure awaits.

5 Must-Visit Restaurants in San Carlos

1. Restaurante Casa Manolo

Perfect for: Romantic dinners, Mediterranean flair, waterfront vibes and beautiful views
Location: Marina San Carlos, above Barracuda Bob's, next to the Yacht Club

Why it stands out:

  • Mediterranean/European style cuisine prepared with local ingredients
  • Seafood pastas, fine cuts of beef, lamb, and more
  • Elegant yet relaxed setting with marina views
  • Takeout and reservations available
Pro Tips for Visitors:
  • Get there in time for sunset if possible — the lighting over the marina makes for beautiful ambiance
  • If you're visiting on a weekend, make sure to have a reservation, especially if you want waterfront seating
  • Try their seafood pasta or a grilled fish dish — both reflect the best of local and Mediterranean fusion

2. Maguro Restaurant & Sushi Bar (Maguro Barra)

Perfect for: Sushi lovers, refined fish dishes, dining in the Marina
Location: Marina San Carlos, at Dock I beside Marvida's

Why it stands out:

  • Japanese/Edomae-style concept with sushi, sashimi, and nigiri
  • Creative seafood dishes highlighting fresh catches
  • Elegant but casual ambiance — ideal for a nicer dinner without overly formal flair
  • Hours cater to both early evening and later diners
Pro Tips for Visitors:
  • Ask for chef's specials or omakase if offered — these often highlight the best catch of the day
  • Pair your sushi with a crisp white wine or sake
  • Take time to stroll around the marina after dinner — the marina lights and boats are part of the charm

3. Sunset Bar & Grill

Perfect for: Beachfront meals, laid-back dinners, watching the sunset
Location: Algodones Beach, KM 20 — right on the sand

Why it stands out:

  • Fresh seafood with Sonoran flair — grilled catches, burgers, arrachera
  • Right on the beach with incredible sunset views
  • Music evenings and entertainment complement the beach setting
  • Creative menu options alongside traditional favorites
Pro Tips for Visitors:
  • Aim for dinner time — as the name suggests, sunset views here are magical
  • Arrive a bit early in peak season to get a good table with beach view
  • Don't skip appetizers or grilled seafood — they often shine more than the main courses

4. Soggy Peso Bar

Perfect for: Casual beach eats, margaritas, relaxed hangouts
Location: New beachfront location beside Playa Blanca condos
Note: Recently reopened as of March 2025

Why it stands out:

  • Retains original relaxed beach bar charm that locals and visitors love
  • Menu includes: crab tostadas, ceviche, shrimp quesadilla, whole grilled fish, seafood fettuccine, 10 oz filet mignon
  • Live music on Friday, Saturday, and Sunday
  • Fun beach-bar games like ring toss
Pro Tips for Visitors:
  • Bring a swimsuit — you can eat with your feet in the sand or wade out for a swim
  • Go mid-afternoon for a relaxed experience before dinner crowds
  • Be adventurous: try the grilled oysters, clams, or one of their signature margaritas

5. Mercado San Carlos

Perfect for: Sampling local flavors, casual eats, exploring many vendors
Location: Blvd. Gabriel Estrada Lt1 in San Carlos
Hours: Open long hours from early morning into late evening

Why it stands out:

  • Food-market style with multiple vendors and stalls
  • Variety of dishes — roam, choose, and sample different cuisines
  • Vibrant and casual atmosphere with authentic local feel
  • Flexible timing — ideal for any meal throughout the day
Pro Tips for Visitors:
  • Come hungry — there's a lot to explore, and you might want to sample from multiple stalls
  • Ask for local recommendations or what's fresh right now — vendors often know best
  • It's a great choice for a quick bite or a relaxed evening when you don't feel like formal dining

Suggested Food Tour Itinerary

Here's a sample way to work these restaurants into a multi-day food tour of San Carlos:

Day Lunch Dinner
Day 1 Mercado San Carlos (casual, variety) Casa Manolo (romantic, upscale)
Day 2 Sunset Bar & Grill (beach lunch) Maguro Barra (sushi dinner)
Day 3 Soggy Peso Bar (beachside midday) Revisit favorite or try local taquerias

Additional Dining Tips

Essential Tips for Your San Carlos Food Tour:
  • Pace yourself: The seafood is rich, and portion sizes can be generous
  • Ask about daily specials or catch-of-the-day: Some restaurants serve items only when fresh
  • Check hours: Some venues close earlier or are closed certain weekdays (e.g. Casa Manolo is closed Mondays)
  • Arrive early for sunset views: The coastal light is part of the experience
